Training Programs for studying Abroad

"Preparing for exams is a crucial step in pursuing education abroad. Universities often require standardized tests like IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, GRE, GMAT, SAT, ACT, or Duolingo English Test to evaluate language proficiency or academic aptitude. These exams assess various skills, such as language proficiency in English or French, and quantitative, analytical, and verbal abilities. Adequate preparation for these exams is essential to meet admission criteria and excel in the academic journey abroad."

IELTS

A widely accepted English proficiency test for assessing language skills.

TOEFL

Recognized by universities worldwide, evaluating English language abilities.

SAT

Used for undergraduate admissions in the United States, assessing mathematical, reading, and writing skills.

GMAT

Typically required for MBA programs, evaluating analytical writing, integrated reasoning, quantitative, and verbal skills.

GRE

Commonly required for graduate programs, assessing analytical writing, quantitative reasoning, and verbal reasoning.

PTE

Another English proficiency test accepted by many universities, assessing listening, reading, speaking, and writing.

ACT

A modern language proficiency test gaining popularity for assessing English language skills, accepted by a growing number of universities as an alternative to traditional exams.

Explore Standardized Tests for Academic Admissions

Here's a brief overview for each test, including test structure and the countries that commonly accept them:

IELTS (International English Language Testing System)

IELTS evaluates proficiency in the English language and is commonly used for academic and immigration purposes.

Sections:
  • Listening: Four sections, 30 minutes.
  • Reading: Three sections, 60 minutes.
  • Writing: Two tasks, 60 minutes.
  • Speaking: Face-to-face interview, 11-14 minutes.
Scoring:

Scores range from 0 to 9 for each section, and the overall band score is an average.

Acceptance:

Widely accepted in English-speaking countries like the UK, Australia, Canada, and the USA.

TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language)

TOEFL assesses English proficiency for academic purposes and is commonly required by universities in English-speaking countries.

Sections:
  • Reading: Three to four passages, 60-80 minutes.
  • Listening: Four to six lectures, 60-90 minutes.
  • Speaking: Six tasks, 20 minutes.
  • Writing: Two tasks, 50 minutes.
Scoring:

Scores range from 0 to 30 for each section, and the total score is out of 120.

Acceptance:

Accepted by institutions in the USA, Canada, Australia, the UK, and many other countries.

PTE (Pearson Test of English)

PTE assesses English language proficiency for academic and immigration purposes.

Sections:
  • Reading: Multiple-choice, re-order paragraphs, 32-41 minutes.
  • Listening: Various question formats, 45-57 minutes.
  • Speaking: Read aloud, repeat sentence, describe image, 25-30 minutes.
  • Writing: Summarize written text, essay, 32-40 minutes.
Scoring:

Scores range from 10 to 90 for each section.

Acceptance:

Widely accepted in English-speaking countries and institutions globally.

GRE (Graduate Record Examination)

GRE assesses readiness for graduate-level study, focusing on critical thinking and analytical skills.

Sections:
  • Analytical Writing: Analyze an issue, analyze an argument, 60 minutes.
  • Verbal Reasoning: Reading comprehension, text completion, sentence equivalence, 60 minutes.
  • Quantitative Reasoning: Problem-solving, data interpretation, 70 minutes.
Scoring:

Analytical Writing scored on a scale of 0 to 6, Verbal and Quantitative on a scale of 130 to 170.

Acceptance:

Accepted by graduate programs globally.

GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test)

GMAT assesses skills essential for success in management and business programs.

Sections:
  • Analytical Writing Assessment: Analysis of an argument, 30 minutes.
  • Integrated Reasoning: Multi-source reasoning, table analysis, 30 minutes.
  • Quantitative: Data sufficiency, problem-solving, 62 minutes.
  • Verbal: Reading comprehension, critical reasoning, sentence correction, 65 minutes.
Scoring:

Total score ranges from 200 to 800.

Acceptance:

Commonly required for MBA programs worldwide.

SAT (Scholastic Assessment Test)

SAT is a standardized test widely used for college admissions in the United States.

Sections:
  • Evidence-Based Reading and Writing: Reading comprehension, writing, 65 minutes.
  • Math: Algebra, problem-solving, data analysis, 80 minutes.
Scoring:

Total score ranges from 400 to 1600.

Acceptance:

Used for undergraduate admissions in the USA.

ACT (American College Testing)

ACT is a standardized test used for college admissions in the United States.

Sections:
  • English: Usage/mechanics, rhetorical skills, 45 minutes.
  • Math: Pre-algebra through trigonometry, 60 minutes.
  • Reading: Interpretation, analysis, evaluation, 35 minutes.
  • Science: Interpretation, analysis, problem-solving, 35 minutes.
Scoring:

Composite score ranges from 1 to 36.

Acceptance:

Commonly used for undergraduate admissions in the USA.

Duolingo English Test

Duolingo is a modern language proficiency test used for academic admissions.

Sections:
  • Reading: Multiple-choice, 45 minutes.
  • Writing: Short-answer questions, 45 minutes.
  • Listening: Multiple-choice, 20 minutes.
  • Speaking: Oral responses, 20 minutes.
Scoring:

Scores range from 10 to 160.

Acceptance:

Accepted by a growing number of universities worldwide.
